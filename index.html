Day 01
PESE Self Introduction
Communication Soft Skills
›
Overview

The art of self-introduction — one of the most essential yet underestimated professional skills. Explored how first impressions are built not just through what you say, but how you say it. Structured around five key parameters that together determine how you land in any professional room.

5 Key Parameters

Effective Communication

Be precise and to the point. Language must reflect clarity and be grammatically correct.

Presentation Skill

Tone, pace, and word choice collectively shape how the audience perceives you.

Self Confidence

Eye contact, upright posture, and a steady voice communicate assurance before you say a word.

Structural Speech

Clear opening — who you are. Purposeful closing — what you bring to the table.

Positive Body Language

Eye contact, a warm smile, and open gestures significantly enhance impact.

Core Takeaway

Self-introduction is not about reciting your resume. It is about controlling the narrative of how others first perceive your competence and character. Every parameter reinforces the others — strong structure with poor confidence, or great words with closed body language, breaks the whole.

Day 06
PESE ATS Resume Writing
Career Resume
›
What Is ATS

Applicant Tracking Systems are the gatekeepers before human eyes ever see your resume. They parse and score based on keyword matches, format compatibility, and structure. A great resume that fails ATS parsing never reaches a recruiter — learned exactly how to make sure yours does.

Rules That Matter
01

Single column only. No tables, graphics, or icons. Use conventional headings like 'Professional Summary' and 'Skills'.

02

Keyword match. Pull exact phrases from the job description. Use bullet lists for skills, not paragraphs.

03

Quantify everything. Not "taught students" — "Taught 120+ students with 95% completion rate."

04

ATS killers: Photos, multi-column layouts, fancy templates, info buried in headers or footers.

Applied Output

The best resume is not the most beautiful one — it is the one that survives the machine and then convinces the human. Format for the parser; content for the person.

Day 07
PESE Group Discussion — Techniques
Teamwork Discussion
›
What GD Tests

Group Discussion rounds evaluate more than what you know — they test how you think collaboratively, handle disagreement, and contribute value in a group setting. GD is not a debate and not a monologue. It is a conversation where your listening matters as much as your speaking.

4 Key Techniques

Quality over Quantity

Fewer meaningful points beat frequent shallow ones. Interviewers notice who adds substance, not volume.

Entry & Engagement

Enter early with a strong point or build on others. Stay visibly engaged even when not speaking.

Respectful Disagreement

Frame it as "I see it differently" — never "You're wrong." Aggression kills credibility instantly.

Summarisation

Closing with a concise, neutral summary signals maturity and strong listening ability to evaluators.

Core Takeaway

The person who controls the summary controls how the GD is remembered. Listening strategically throughout is what makes a strong close possible — it is not improvised, it is built through attention.
